Getting Started with the Library

1
Understanding the Models
Why "Clearance Bricks" are better

This master document is designed to be the ultimate reference library for anyone building custom 3D printers, toolheads, or motion systems. Instead of hunting down dozens of obscure datasheets or downloading heavily detailed step files that crash your CAD software, everything here is built as lightweight "clearance models."

💡 Pro Tip for Onshape Users

If you use Onshape, you don't need to download anything. Just click "Insert" in your own Assembly, navigate to "Other Documents," paste the library URL, and drop the parts straight into your build.

⚠️ Performance Warning

These models intentionally omit complex internal geometry like bearing tracks, motor coils, or fan blades. They are strictly for layout, clearance checking, and assembly mapping. Do not try to use these to manufacture replacement internals!

2
Using Configurations
One file, dozens of variations

The real magic of this library is the configuration system. For components that share the same basic shape, I have built fully automated, parametric models. Instead of navigating through folders containing 21 different axial fans or 8 different linear rails, you only deal with a single configurable part.

How to change a part size:

  • Step 1: Open the Part Studio for the component type you need (e.g., NEMA Motors, Axial Fans, MGN Carriages).
  • Step 2: Look at the top-left corner of the viewport for the Configuration Dropdown menu.
  • Step 3: Select your exact spec from the list (like swapping from a 2510 Axial Fan to a 14025). The 3D model, mounting holes, and part names will instantly update.

ℹ️ Note on Asymmetrical Parts

Some parts, like Radial Blowers, are mapped as individual models in their own folders rather than configurations. This is because their shapes and mounting tabs vary so drastically between sizes (e.g., a 5015 vs a 12032) that forcing them into one configuration breaks the geometry. If a part doesn't have a dropdown, check its specific folder!

Exporting to Other CAD Software
For Fusion360, SolidWorks, and FreeCAD users

If you prefer to work in offline CAD software, you can easily pull files from this document. Because the parts are parametric, you will need to export the specific size you require.

How to Export a STEP File:

  • 1. Open the public link and navigate to the Part Studio you need.
  • 2. If the part is configurable (like the Axial Fans), use the drop-down menu in the top left to select your exact size.
  • 3. Look at the bottom-left corner of your screen for the Parts folder.
  • 4. Right-click the part name (e.g., "Fan - Axial - 4020") and select Export.
  • 5. Choose "STEP" as your format and hit download!

Contributing to the Project
Help build the ultimate maker library

The 3D printing space moves fast, and new hotends, extruders, and boards are released constantly. Building out the Hotend and Extruder sections takes a massive amount of time for one person, so community contributions are incredibly welcome!

Contribution Rules:

To keep the library legally clean and performant, any submitted models must adhere to two strict rules:

  • Original Models Only: You must model the part yourself using reference dimensions or calipers. We absolutely cannot accept step files downloaded from other file-sharing sites (like Printables, MakerWorld, or GrabCAD) due to licensing restrictions.
  • Keep it Simple (Clearance Bricks): Do not model internal gears, complex cooling fins, or PCB traces. The models must be "dumbed down" to their bare external dimensions, mounting holes, and critical clearances. Complex geometry crashes large assemblies!

ℹ️ How to Submit

If you have modeled a new popular component following the rules above, please reach out! You can use the contact form on my main website at kanrog.com, or email me directly at kanrogcreations@gmail.com. Send a link to your public Onshape document, and if it meets the standards, it will be merged into the master library!
